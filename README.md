# 💫 About Me:
## About Me<br><br>I started my journey in tech with web development, building websites using ASP.NET with a C++ backend and MySQL databases. That early experience taught me how systems work end to end, and it gave me a strong foundation to build on.<br><br>As I grew more curious about intelligent systems, I transitioned into Python and quickly fell in love with what it could do. I completed an internship where I worked with FastAPI and Django, building backend services and APIs. From there, I moved into a Machine Learning internship, where I got hands-on experience building real models, cleaning data, and understanding how algorithms learn from examples. This naturally led me to an Artificial Intelligence internship, where I worked on more advanced AI systems and started connecting the dots between theory and real-world applications.<br><br>To validate my growing skills, I earned the Microsoft Azure AI Fundamentals certification, which deepened my understanding of cloud-based AI services and responsible AI practices.<br><br>With that background, I took a big step and got admitted to Sheffield Hallam University to pursue my MSc in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics. University opened up a whole new world for me. In my first semester, I worked on a Video Storytelling Score Prediction model using deep learning and LSTMs, predicting how well a video tells a story by analysing sequences and patterns. I also built projects in OpenCV for real-time computer vision and explored Natural Language Processing, which made me realise how broad and exciting AI really is. Robotics caught my attention in a big way too, combining hardware, sensors, and intelligent decision-making into one fascinating field.<br><br>While studying, I landed a job as an AI Engineer at Ladybird in the UK, working on the NHS InCall Project. My main role is building and improving a Retrieval-Augmented Generation system that helps a voice AI pull accurate medical knowledge to assist healthcare professionals over phone calls. The system handles over 1000 simultaneous calls with response times under 500ms, and I have been actively working on making it faster, smarter, and more reliable. Beyond RAG, I am now exploring video generation, looking at how AI can create and understand visual content automatically.<br><br>In my second semester at Sheffield Hallam, I started working with Reachy Mini, a real physical robot. My contribution to the project focuses on the vision and knowledge side. I am using YOLO for real-time object detection, giving the robot the ability to see and identify objects around it, and integrating ConceptNet to help the robot understand what those objects mean and how they relate to the world. This work sits at the heart of building robots that do not just see, but actually understand their environment.<br><br>Looking back, my journey has taken me from building websites to training neural networks, from Python internships to deploying AI systems used in real UK healthcare, and from classroom robotics projects to working with a real physical robot. Every step has added a new layer to how I think about building intelligent systems, and I am only getting started.
